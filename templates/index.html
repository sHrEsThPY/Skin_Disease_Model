7 Conditions We Detect
All conditions are from the HAM10000 clinical dermoscopy dataset.
Precancerous

Actinic Keratosis

AK / Solar Keratosis

Rough, scaly sun-damaged patches that can progress to squamous cell carcinoma if untreated.

Malignant

Basal Cell Carcinoma

BCC — Most Common Skin Cancer

Slow-growing cancer from basal cells. Rarely metastasises but causes local tissue damage.

Benign

Benign Keratosis

Seborrheic Keratosis / BKL

Harmless waxy growths that appear "stuck on" the skin. Common with ageing, no treatment required.

Benign

Dermatofibroma

Fibrous Histiocytoma / DF

Hard, firm nodule under the skin from accumulated fibroblasts. Dimples inward when pinched.

High Risk

Melanoma

Malignant Melanoma

The deadliest skin cancer. Develops from melanocytes and can rapidly metastasise to organs.

Benign

Melanocytic Nevi

Common Mole / NV

Clusters of melanocytes. Most are harmless but should be monitored for changes (ABCDE rule).

Benign

Vascular Lesion

Cherry Angioma / VASC

Blood vessel-derived lesions ranging from cherry angiomas to angiokeratomas. Generally benign.
